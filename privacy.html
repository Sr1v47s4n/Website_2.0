Privacy Policy

1. Information We Collect

We collect information directly from you when you register for events, subscribe to newsletters, or contact us for support. This may include your name, email address, phone number, and any other details you provide.

2. How We Use Information

We use the information we collect for several purposes, including:

  • To provide and maintain our service
  • To notify you about changes to our service
  • To allow you to participate in interactive features of our website when you choose to do so.
  • To provide customer support
  • To gather analysis or valuable information so that we can improve our service
  • To monitor the usage of our service
  • To detect, prevent and address technical issues

3. Disclosure of Information

  • We do not share your personal information with third parties.
  • We may disclose your personal information for any other purpose with your consent.
  • We may disclose your personal information if required by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities.

    • 4. Data Security

    We value your trust in providing us your personal information, thus we are striving to use commercially acceptable means of protecting it. But remember that no method of transmission over the internet, or method of electronic storage is 100% secure and reliable, and we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

  • We have the right to update or change our Privacy Policy at any time. Your continued use of the Service after we post any modifications to the Privacy Policy on this page will constitute your acknowledgment of the modifications and your consent to abide and be bound by the modified Privacy Policy.

We have no control over, and assume no responsibility for, the content, privacy policies, or practices of any third-party websites or services. We advise you to review the privacy policy of every site you visit.

    6. Security Testing and Prohibited Activities

  • Prohibited Activities: Security testing, bug bounty programs, or any other form of testing, including but not limited to Denial of Service (DoS) or Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks, are strictly prohibited without explicit written approval from the Energy Club.
